Final notes

  • Very responsive and forthcoming with requests for more information. Even spontaniously offered to provide data of additional unpublished crosses.

  • They build upon the Gantz and Bier 2015 yellow-drive and make a lot of references to it without addressing the issues in that paper (Maternal deposition confused for near-perfect inheritance of unmarked drive). For example: “It should be noted that these percentages are consistent with previously reported yMCR inheritance patterns in D. melanogaster (Champer et al., 2018, 2017; Gantz and Bier, 2015; Xu et al., 2020).”

  • They mention zygotic allelic conversion, which was proposed in the 2015 Gantz and Bier paper and is probably not a thing.

  • The text could use another revision for clarity: Acronyms used before they are defined (e.g., RNP). Some things explained excessively, others not at all.

  • They spend a lot of space in the results, arguing that the yReMEDE cut rate probably isn’t completely zero. The distinction between zero and massively reduced doesn’t matter much.

  • Very loose use of “wild-type”. For example: “However, wild-type alleles containing a wild-type TGG or gGG sequence (possibly produced through erroneous repair) were also present (Fig. 2E, S3 and Data S1).”

  • They never make clear what they want and how that is different from what will happen anyway with resistance to the drive. I assume the original goal was to make the SSA reversion to WT fully inducible with no background rate, but this is never really made explicit.

  • The recoding of the PAM of the newly created “wild-type allele” is given very little emphasis, and it could be argued they are misleading readers (e.g.”the potential to restore the original wild-type population”). On Twitter, they describe the paper as “scar-free removal of a gene drive element”. A casual reader could come always thinking they only used the PAM scar to identify SSA events. However, it is fundamental to the design and has important implications for sequence conservation and the whole point of doing this at all. They themselves show the yMCR drive results in the same outcome (eventual replacement by type-1 resistance alleles).

  • They do not really describe the nuances of the application of this approach. When and how should we use it? For example, I don’t think makes sense for a suppression drive.

  • No mention of the many other repeates in the drive element (3x3P EGFP/3x3P RFP, gyp/gyp, gRNA spacer/target sites) or a discussion of the unintended gRNA recombination seen in other drives (gRNA spacer/target sites).

  • No mention of partial homing. SSA could just as well be homing of the PAM recoding flanked by 250bp of homologous sequences in the DR.

  • The why of each simulation could be better explained. It is mostly left to the reader to piece together what the change in parameters means.
